Overview

What is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence is a set of technologies that enable computers to perform a variety of advanced functions, including the ability to see, understand and translate spoken and written language, analyze data, make recommendations and more. It is focused mainly on machine learning and deep learning, used for data processing, natural language processing, requirements, interpretation and more.

How It Works

In order to learn from the patterns and features in the data they study, artificial intelligence (AI) systems combine big data sets with clever, iterative processing methods. Every time an AI system processes data, it evaluates and assesses its own performance and gains new knowledge. AI can complete hundreds, thousands, or even millions of tasks very fast since it never requires a break. It can learn a lot in a short period of time and become very good at whatever it is being trained to do. However, the key to comprehending how AI actually functions is realizing that it is a whole field, or a science, rather than only a single computer program or application. . Building a computer system that can simulate human behavior in order to solve complicated issues using human-like thought processes is the aim of artificial intelligence (AI) science. AI systems use a wide range of methods and procedures in addition to a huge number of distinct technologies to achieve this goal.

An artificial neural network is a popular kind of AI training model that is somewhat modeled after the human brain. A neural network is a collection of synthetic neurons, also known as perceptrons, which are computational nodes used for data analysis and classification. Each perceptron in the first layer of a neural network receives the data, makes a judgment, and then relays that information to several nodes in the subsequent layer. "Deep neural networks" or "deep learning" are terms used to describe training models that have more than three layers. There are hundreds or thousands of layers in some contemporary neural networks. The neural network's task, such as classifying an object or identifying patterns in data, is completed by the output of the last perceptrons.

Advantages

Eliminates human error and risk

One of the core advantages of AI is that it aids in reducing human error, as well as risk to humans. It is normal to make mistakes as humans which is not really a problem. However, when it comes to striving for a complete result, it can be become a big deal. Using Ai to complete tasks, can prevent error from ruining an otherwise perfect essential product. Likewise, the use of AI can prevent humans from undergoing difficult or risky tasks that would likely damage their body systems. Example is a high- radiated area. Unlike humans that would get seriously sick or even die from the radiation, robots can easily survive them. If results are fatal, robots can easily be repaired.

Unlimited Availability

As humans, we easily get tired after long hours of work, study or just being physically active. AI programs, on the other hand, are always available no matter how long they have worked. They have the ability to provide customer service even outside work hours. As a result, companies have the ability to develop and provide a better customer experience than how they usually produce when they are inhabited by only humans.

Unbiased decision making

Humans usually have different opinions which can contribute to the rise of bias especially within a particular company. When one makes decisions and the other gives a counterargument, that would lead up to bias of two individuals. On the other hand, provided the AI algorithm has been trained using unbiased datasets and tested for programming bias, the program will be able to make decisions without the influence of bias. That can help provide more equity in things like selecting job applications, approving loans, or credit applications. Though if the AI was created using biased datasets or training data it can make biased decisions that aren’t caught because people assume the decisions are unbiased. That’s why quality checks are essential on the training data, as well as the results that a specific AI program produces to ensure that bias issues aren’t overlooked.

Accurate medical diagnosis

12,629 clinical claims for medical negligence were filed against the NHS in 2020–2021, a 133% rise from 2006–2007. This underscores the urgent need for innovation in the healthcare industry. Over the last 50 years, medical researchers have made huge advancements in leveraging AI for more accurate diagnosis – and treatment – of disease. They have solved integration problems and built upon early rule-based systems to create a model that can perform this vital task as accurately as people, if not more so. Early signs already suggest significant advancements; experts assert that using AI to analyze and examine radiography and mammography images can speed up the procedure by at least 30 times while maintaining 99% accuracy. For example: Google’s DeepMind is actively involved in Deep Learning technology to fill gaps across different sectors. Part of this initiative – a collaborative effort with Moorfields Eye Hospital – is training a neural network to analyse 3D retinal scans to detect more than 50 types of eye disease.

Disadvantages

Despite its benefits, AI also have some serious side effects individuals should be warned about.

Difficult to Understand

Most AI models including ones that rely heavily on technology can be difficult to understand. This results in unsolved mysteries surrounding how AI comes up with a conclusion. A biggest concern with AI is that companies are unaware of potential threats, making it difficult for lawmakers to ensure responsive use of AI.

High Unemployment Rate

The increase of AI- powered job automation raises concern to employees of different companies as the technology is slowly taking over industries such as: stock markets, healthcare and manufacturing facilities. With AI robots developing, the same tasks would mean fewer humans. Even though AI is set to create high amount of new jobs in 2025, many employees will not have developed enough skills needed for such roles which would lead to them being fired and later replaced by AI as a company's strategy of making money.

Lack of Data Privacy Using AI tools

AI tools contain large amounts of data without any regard for security. AI systems like Generative AI, when misused, collect personal data to customize user experiences which are not even secure as the technology allows hackers to access an individual's personal account including private information such as password, address, etc, possibly leading to data loss. Their ability to process and generate sensitive information can be exploited for malicious purposes, such as phishing or creating convincing spam messages. This became evident when an Large Language model was used to craft sophisticated phishing emails that successfully bypassed traditional security filters. This example highlights the need for robust security measures to protect against the misuse of LLMs and safeguard sensitive data.

Environmental Impact

While it is true that AI can provide employees with a healthy working environment, It can also cause some significant negative impact on the environment. Because AI systems rely heavily on electricity, they give rise to excess energy consumption. Individuals with economic hardships are the most impacted because they might already be dealing with several challenges like insufficient funds, inflation, with electricity bills thrown in the mix.

Over-Reliance of Technology

When you are over dependent on AI, you are under a risk of losing interest in showcasing your talent. For instance, you rely on chatgpt, you end up losing your ability to socialise physically with other people. Likewise when a company replaces humans with AI, many people begin to lose their jobs because of this which would likely weaken their productivity. Another instance is the use of AI in homes. Parents should play a part in their children's lives because children typically look up to them and would love to spend time with them. When they rely heavily on AI, the children's development skills might suffer as parents depend on technology to take care of their kids for them. AI is not capable of teaching them what is right and what is wrong; moreover, they are not as supportive as humans as they disregard a child's safety. Some children might begin to feel lonely and eventually lose their eagerness to play around with other kids since they have nobody to depend on even their parents.

Lack of Accountability and Responsibility

When an AI system makes a decision, even risky ones, it is difficult to determine who is to blame for damages caused in a particular sector: AI developer, company or the technology itself? This lack of accountability becomes a problem in certain places like hospitals, banks, etc, where lives are the most impacted due to the decisions made.

Laziness

Since AI is capable of managing any level of task, people become increasingly lazy. This is evident among students as AI systems provide them with necessary information about their assigned task. As a result, they no longer make enough effort to complete their work, rather they rely on AI to finish their assessments for them. This can also be found among companies as employees depend on AI so much that they begin to develop a habit of slacking off and ditching work allowing AI to do all their duties. This can also be evident in homes as AI handles house chores without a human having to lift a finger.
